The Electoral College - SI649

In most elections, the candidate with the most votes wins. This is called Proportional Representation, each voter has the same power! But for presidential elections in the US, the Electoral College system is used instead. In this system, instead of counting the popular vote, we count the number of "Electoral College Votes" and each state gets a different number of Electoral Votes based on their population size.

In the Electoral College System, every state is garunteed 3 Electoral Votes regardless of the states' population. After that additional electoral votes are distributed based on on states' populations. This means that states that less populous states, especially those that never qualified for additional electoral votes, often have less voters per electoral college voters. This relationship between total voters and electoral college voters is called "Voter Power"
Voting Power = (# of Votes)/(# of Electoral Votes)
